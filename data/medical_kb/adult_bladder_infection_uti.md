# Adult Bladder Infection (UTI)

A bladder infection is a common type of urinary tract infection. It is usually caused by bacteria entering the bladder and multiplying there.

Typical symptoms include burning during urination, feeling the need to urinate often, lower abdominal pain or discomfort, and urine that looks cloudy, has blood in it, or has a strong smell.

Health professionals diagnose bladder infections using medical history, physical exam, and lab testing. In some cases, additional tests may be needed to look for a cause or contributing problem.

Treatment most often involves antibiotics. The source also notes that drinking more liquids may help recovery and ease symptoms. Water is the preferred fluid. If untreated, a bladder infection can progress to a kidney infection.

The source notes that diet itself is not a treatment for bladder infection, although hydration can help prevention and recovery.

This document supports patient-education questions about common lower-UTI symptoms, expected treatment direction, and the reason symptoms should not be ignored.
