# Viral Gastroenteritis

Viral gastroenteritis is an infection of the intestines. People often call it "stomach flu," but the source states that this term is medically inaccurate because influenza viruses do not cause viral gastroenteritis.

Typical symptoms include watery diarrhea, abdominal pain or cramping, nausea, vomiting, and sometimes fever. The illness spreads through contact with small particles of an infected person’s stool or vomit.

Doctors often diagnose viral gastroenteritis from symptoms alone if the illness is mild and short-lived. Tests are not always needed.

In most cases, treatment focuses on replacing lost fluids and electrolytes to prevent dehydration. When appetite comes back, many people can return to a normal diet even if diarrhea is still present. The source does not recommend fasting or a highly restricted diet in most cases.

Prevention measures include handwashing, disinfecting contaminated surfaces, and, in children, protection from rotavirus through vaccination.

This document is useful for questions about vomiting-and-diarrhea illnesses, the difference between "stomach flu" and influenza, and the importance of hydration.
