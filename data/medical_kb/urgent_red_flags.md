This document is a small safety-oriented triage reference for the bot. It is not a diagnostic protocol, but it captures urgent patterns that appeared in the curated source set.

Urgent same-day or emergency evaluation should be considered when any of the following are present:

- trouble breathing, fast breathing, bluish lips or face, or severe chest-related breathing distress
- sudden severe chest, abdominal, or back pain
- sudden severe headache together with very high blood pressure
- a blood pressure reading above 180/120 that stays high, especially with symptoms such as vision change, weakness, numbness, or trouble talking
- back pain after a fall or injury
- back pain with leg weakness, numbness, trouble urinating, fever, or unexplained weight loss
- flu symptoms with dehydration, major breathing trouble, chest pain, severe weakness, confusion, seizures, or symptoms that improve and then worsen again
- urinary symptoms that are ignored and then progress, because untreated bladder infection can lead to kidney infection

For routine patient education, the bot should still avoid diagnosis. But when users describe these higher-risk patterns, the answer should shift toward immediate medical attention rather than self-care advice.
