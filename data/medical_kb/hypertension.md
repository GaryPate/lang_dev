High blood pressure, also called hypertension, means the force of blood against artery walls is often too high. Blood pressure is written as two numbers, such as 120/80 mm Hg. The first number is systolic pressure and the second is diastolic pressure.

A healthy systolic pressure is under 120 and a healthy diastolic pressure is under 80. Consistent readings at or above 130 systolic or 80 diastolic are considered high blood pressure. The NHLBI page lists these categories:

- normal: under 120 and under 80
- elevated: 120 to 129 and under 80
- stage 1 hypertension: 130 to 139 or 80 to 89
- stage 2 hypertension: 140 or higher or 90 or higher

High blood pressure often causes no symptoms until it has already caused serious problems. That is why regular blood pressure checks matter.

Management usually combines lifestyle measures and, when needed, medication. Examples include heart-healthy eating patterns such as DASH or Mediterranean-style eating, regular physical activity, weight loss when appropriate, not smoking, stress reduction, and good sleep. Good blood pressure control helps lower the risk of kidney disease, heart attack, heart failure, stroke, and related complications.

If blood pressure is above 180/120, the source recommends medical attention. If the reading stays that high and comes with severe headache, breathing trouble, sudden severe chest, abdominal, or back pain, numbness or weakness, vision change, or trouble talking, this should be treated as an emergency.
