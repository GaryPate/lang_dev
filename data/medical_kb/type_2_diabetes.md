# Type 2 Diabetes

Type 2 diabetes is the most common form of diabetes. It happens when blood glucose stays too high because the body does not make enough insulin or does not use insulin well.

Symptoms can include increased thirst, increased urination, increased hunger, tiredness, blurred vision, numbness or tingling in the hands or feet, sores that heal slowly, and unexplained weight loss. Symptoms may develop slowly over years and can be mild enough that some people do not notice them. Some people only learn they have diabetes after complications appear.

Risk is higher with older age, family history, overweight or obesity, physical inactivity, prediabetes, and some prior pregnancy-related diabetes histories. High blood pressure is also one of the related risk factors listed by the source.

Diagnosis is based on blood tests. Management usually focuses on blood glucose control, blood pressure control, cholesterol management, smoking cessation, physical activity, healthy meal planning, and prescribed medicines when needed. The source emphasizes working with a health care team on a diabetes care plan.

For prevention or delay of type 2 diabetes in people with risk factors, the source highlights weight loss when appropriate, regular physical activity, healthier food choices, and replacing sugary drinks with water.
