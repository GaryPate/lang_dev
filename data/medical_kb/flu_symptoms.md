# Flu Symptoms

Flu is a contagious respiratory illness caused by influenza viruses. It can be mild or severe, and in some cases it can lead to life-threatening complications.

Flu symptoms usually start suddenly. Common symptoms include fever or chills, cough, sore throat, runny or stuffy nose, muscle or body aches, headache, and fatigue. Some people also have vomiting or diarrhea, which is more common in children than adults. Not everyone with flu has a fever.

Compared with a common cold, flu is more likely to start abruptly and to cause stronger body aches, chills, fatigue, headaches, and a more severe cough. The source notes that colds more often develop gradually.

Most people recover within a few days to less than two weeks, but complications can occur. These include pneumonia, worsening of asthma or heart disease, muscle inflammation, heart inflammation, brain inflammation, kidney failure, respiratory failure, and sepsis.

People at higher risk of serious complications include adults 65 and older, children under 5 years old, pregnant people, and people with chronic conditions such as asthma, diabetes, or heart disease.

This document is useful for symptom recognition, cold-versus-flu questions, and identifying when flu may be more serious than a routine viral upper-respiratory illness.
